## 🎮 Game Basics

**What**: You're running a tech startup. Make decisions. Face consequences.

**Who**: Teams of 4–5 students on one device

**How Long**: 20–30 minutes

**Goal**: Not to "win," but to think about trade-offs

---

## 📊 Four Metrics (What Matters)

### 📈 IMPACT  
*How fast did you solve the problem?*  
- Fast decisions = high impact  
- Thorough decisions = lower impact  

### ❤️ INCLUSIVITY  
*Did you care about ALL users?*  
- Including people with disabilities = high inclusivity  
- Rush to launch = lower inclusivity  

### 🤝 TRUST  
*Do people believe in you?*  
- Honesty during crises = high trust  
- Hiding problems = low trust  

### 💰 BUDGET  
*Do you have money left?*  
- Good decisions cost money  
- If it hits £0, game ends  

---

## ⚠️ Key Rules

✅ **Discuss every decision** with your team (30–60 seconds per choice)

✅ **Everyone gets a vote** on what to do

✅ **There's no right answer** — different choices lead to different outcomes

✅ **You'll face a crisis** — every game has 1 unexpected emergency

✅ **Spend carefully** — you start with a limited budget

❌ **Don't be the only one clicking** — take turns controlling the device

---

## 💡 Decision Tips

### When choosing an option, ask:
1. **"Is this fast or thorough?"** (Impact)
2. **"Does this help everyone or just some people?"** (Inclusivity)
3. **"Are we being honest?"** (Trust)
4. **"Can we afford this?"** (Budget)

### Remember:
- Speed costs inclusivity  
- Inclusion costs money  
- Honesty builds trust  
- Crises destroy trust FAST  

---

## 🎯 Winning Strategy?

**There isn't one!**

Different teams WIN by:
- Going **super fast** → high Impact, lower Inclusivity
- Being **super ethical** → high Inclusivity, lower Budget  
- **Balancing** everything → all metrics 60–70
- **Taking risks** → some high, some low
- **Playing it safe** → never run out of money

**What matters**: Making thoughtful choices & being able to explain WHY

---

## 📋 Day Order (What to Expect)

1. **Idea Day** — "What problem do we solve?"
2. **Build Day** — "How do we make it?"
3. **Launch Day** — "How do we tell users?"
4. **⚠️ CRISIS DAY** — "OH NO! Something went wrong!"
5. **Growth Day** — "How do we expand?"

*(Each game has 5–7 days, so some might skip)*

---

## 🚨 When Crisis Hits

**Types of crises** (surprise!):
- 📱 Data breach — hackers stole user data!
- ♿ Accessibility complaint — app doesn't work for disabled users
- 🤖 AI bias — algorithm treats some people unfairly
- 👥 Company scandal — workplace culture problem

**How to respond**: 
- Fast + honest = restore some trust
- Fast + cover-up = lose LOTS of trust
- Slow response = crisis gets worse

---

## ✅ Team Tips

**Before the game:**
- Agree on your team values: "What matters most to us?"
- Assign roles: Who speaks? Who clicks? Who takes notes?

**During the game:**
- **Discuss**: Don't let one person decide everything
- **Disagree respectfully**: "I prefer option A because..."
- **Keep track**: Write down each choice (useful for debrief)
- **Ask questions**: If the text is confusing, ask your teacher

**After the game:**
- Celebrate your scores (whatever they are!)
- Compare with other teams: "Why did you choose differently?"
- Reflect: "Would you do it again the same way?"

---

## 🤔 Good Discussion Starters

### For your team:
- *"Which option is faster but riskier?"*
- *"Who does this decision affect?"*
- *"What would REAL companies do?"*
- *"If money wasn't limited, would we choose differently?"*

### For debrief:
- *"What surprised you about the results?"*
- *"Was the 'ethical' choice also smart business?"*
- *"Did your budget run out? Why?"*
- *"Would you make different choices next time?"*
