## 📋 Running a Class Session (Step-by-Step)

### Before Class

1. **Choose a seed** (or leave it random)
2. **Test the scenario** yourself
   - Open the game in your browser
   - Play through to see what students will face
   - Note interesting decision points to highlight
3. **Read the "How to Play" page** to answer student questions
4. **Prepare your debrief questions** (see below)

### During Class

**Setup Phase (5 minutes)**
- Explain: "You're running a tech startup. Every decision affects 4 metrics."
- Have teams pick a team name
- If using a seed, show students the URL or distribute it
- Have one person be the **Decision Keeper** (reads options aloud)

**Gameplay Phase (15–20 minutes)**
- Teams huddle around one device
- Discuss each decision for 30–60 seconds before choosing
- Remind them: "There's no right answer—what matters is your team's reasoning"
- Walk around and listen to team discussions
- Note interesting choices or disagreements for debrief

**Results Phase (3 minutes)**
- Show final scores and outcome message
- Let teams see: Impact, Inclusivity, Trust, Budget

### After Class Debrief (10–15 minutes)

**Discuss the Decisions**
- "What was the toughest decision you made?"
- "Did any outcome surprise you?"
- "Would you make different choices knowing the results?"

**Compare Across Teams** (if using same seed)
- "Team A chose X, Team B chose Y. What were the consequences?"
- "Which team made better decisions about budget?"
- "Which team prioritized inclusivity most?"

**Connect to Real World**
- "Google faced an accessibility crisis in 2021. How would you have responded?"
- "Facebook's data breaches cost them billions in fines. Why does trust matter?"
- "Companies like Apple say they're 'privacy-first.' What does that mean in their decisions?"

**Ethics Discussion**
- "Is it okay to delay accessibility if it means launching faster?"
- "Should companies care about inclusivity if profit isn't affected?"
- "When should you be transparent vs. strategic during a crisis?"

**Reflection** (journal or discussion)
- "What did you learn about running a company?"
- "Which metric (Impact, Inclusivity, Trust, Budget) is hardest to balance?"
- "Would you want to start a tech company? Why or why not?"

## 🎮 Game Modes & Activities

### Mode 1: Free Play (No Seed)
**What**: Each team gets different scenarios
**Why**: Encourages diverse thinking
**Best For**: Introducing the game

**Debrief**:
- "Did different teams get different days?"
- "How did your scenarios differ?"

---

### Mode 2: Unified Play (With Seed)
**What**: All teams play the same scenarios
**Why**: Direct comparison of decision-making
**Best For**: Teaching decision-making frameworks

**Setup**: Use a seed (e.g., `seed=2024`)

**Debrief**:
- Create a chart of each team's choices for each day
- Compare final scores
- Discuss why same decisions led to different outcomes (random variation doesn't apply with same scenario)

---

### Mode 3: Crisis Focus (Specific Seed)
**What**: Design session around one type of crisis
**Why**: Deep dive into specific issue
**Best For**: Exploring one topic thoroughly

**Example Activity**:
1. Give all teams the same seed with a **data breach crisis**
2. Before playing, ask: "How would YOU respond to a data breach?"
3. Play the game
4. Compare their answer to the most trusted/impactful choice
5. Discuss: "Why is transparency important even if it costs more?"

---

### Mode 4: Ethics Debate (Two Seeds)
**What**: Different groups play different scenarios with same crisis type
**Why**: Compare how different team types handle same problem
**Best For**: Ethics & decision-making practice

**Setup**:
- Group A: Seed 1001 (data breach)
- Group B: Seed 1003 (AI bias)
- Create scenarios with different crisis types

**Activity**:
1. Groups play independently
2. Come together to present their crisis response
3. Vote on: "Which team handled their crisis better? Why?"
4. Discuss: "Is one crisis harder than another?"

## 📊 Scoring Reference

### What Score Ranges Mean

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0–20 | Minimal/poor in this area |
| 21–40 | Below average, has gaps |
| 41–60 | Average, acceptable |
| 61–80 | Good, strong in this area |
| 81–100 | Excellent, prioritized throughout |

### Healthy Balanced Outcome
- **Impact**: 60–75 (solving problems reasonably well)
- **Inclusivity**: 60–75 (thinking about all users)
- **Trust**: 60–75 (being honest and transparent)
- **Budget**: £2,000–£10,000 (having money left, but spent resources)

### Different Playstyles

**Speed-First Team** (prioritizes Impact)
- High Impact (75+)
- Low Inclusivity (40–50)
- Moderate Trust (50–60)
- Low Budget (negative or near zero)
- *Lesson*: Fast decisions save money but damage trust

**Values-First Team** (prioritizes Inclusivity)
- Moderate Impact (50–60)
- High Inclusivity (80+)
- High Trust (75+)
- Very Low Budget (negative)
- *Lesson*: Doing right by users is expensive but builds loyalty

**Balanced Team**
- All metrics 60–70
- Healthy Budget (£5,000–£15,000)
- *Lesson*: Balance is possible but requires careful choices

## 🎬 Sample Class Narratives

### Lesson 1: Introduction to Decision-Making (45 minutes)

**Objectives**: Understand trade-offs, explore decision frameworks

1. **Opening** (5 min): Show a company crisis in the news (e.g., Netflix password sharing)
2. **How to Play** (5 min): Walk through rules quickly
3. **Gameplay** (20 min): Free play, no seed
4. **Debrief** (10 min):
   - "What was the hardest choice?"
   - "Did speed and quality compete?"
   - "Which metric surprised you?"
5. **Wrap-up** (5 min): "Running a company means balancing many things at once."

---

### Lesson 2: Comparing Strategies (50 minutes)

**Objectives**: Understand that different strategies lead to different outcomes

1. **Setup** (5 min): Explain that all teams will play the same scenario (use a seed)
2. **Gameplay** (20 min): Play with `seed=2024`
3. **Data Collection** (5 min):
   - Chart each team's scores on a board
   - Note which team chose most ethical options
4. **Analysis** (15 min):
   - "Why did everyone make different choices?"
   - "Did the 'best' choices come from the same value system?"
   - "Can we rank outcomes? Is one path better than another?"
5. **Reflection** (5 min): "In real life, there's often no single 'right' answer."

---

### Lesson 3: Crisis Management (50 minutes)

**Objectives**: Practice handling unexpected challenges with transparency

1. **Context** (5 min): "Every company faces crises. How do you respond?"
2. **Gameplay** (20 min): Use a seed with a **clear crisis focus** (`seed=1002` for accessibility)
3. **Crisis Analysis** (10 min):
   - "What was the problem?"
   - "How did your choices affect trust?"
   - "Was the most ethical choice also the best business choice?"
4. **Real-World Connection** (10 min):
   - "Google was sued for accessibility issues. What would you do?"
   - "Meta lost billions over data privacy. Was it worth the cost upfront?"
5. **Team Reflection** (5 min): "Crisis management is about values + speed."

---

### Lesson 4: Ethics Debate (60 minutes)

**Objectives**: Explore the ethics of tech company decisions

1. **Preparation** (5 min): Introduce the ethics question
   - "Is inclusivity always worth the cost?"
   - "Should you hide a security breach to avoid panic?"
   - "Can you profit while treating all users fairly?"

2. **Gameplay** (20 min): Two groups play same seed, stop at the crisis stage

3. **Pause at Crisis** (5 min):
   - Before revealing impact, ask: "What SHOULD the company do?"
   - Have teams vote on the most ethical choice

4. **Reveal & Compare** (10 min):
   - Show what each option does to scores
   - Discuss: "Does the ethical choice also produce the best outcomes?"

5. **Debate** (15 min):
   - Pro: "Companies MUST prioritize ethics even if it costs profits"
   - Con: "Companies need to be profitable to survive and help anyone"
   - Medium: "Find the balance"

6. **Reflection** (5 min): "Real-world decisions aren't black and white."

## ❓ Common Student Questions

**Q: "Is there a winning score?"**
A: No! Different approaches (speed, ethics, balance) all lead to different but valid outcomes. What matters is that you made thoughtful choices.

**Q: "Why does accessibility cost so much?"**
A: In real life, retrofitting accessibility is expensive. Building it in from the start is cheaper. It's about planning ahead.

**Q: "Can we get all metrics to 100?"**
A: No—achieving that would require unlimited budget! Real life is about prioritizing what matters most to you.

**Q: "Is there a crisis in every game?"**
A: Yes! Every scenario includes exactly one crisis day. That's real: companies always face unexpected problems.

**Q: "Why did Team A and Team B get different days?"**
A: Without a seed, each playthrough generates new random stages. That's why different teams see different challenges!

## 🔧 Technical Troubleshooting for Teachers

### I want to test a specific seed
```
1. Use URL: http://domain/frontend/select-problem.html?seed=1234
2. Play through and note which crisis appears
3. Screenshot the scenario selection page
4. Write down what makes it unique
```

### I want to find a seed with a specific crisis type
```
1. Try seeds: 1001, 1002, 1003, 1004, 1005...
2. Play through each
3. Note which seed generates your desired crisis
4. Share that seed for consistent classroom use
```

### I want to make the game easier/harder
**Easier**:
- Fewer days: Modify `stageGenerator.js` line with `numStages = 4 + ...`
- Larger initial budget: Check game state initialization
- Easier choices: Look for options with balanced effects

**Harder**:
- More days: Change `numStages` to `6 + Math.floor(...)`
- Smaller initial budget: Reduce starting amount in gameState
- Harsher consequences: Increase negative effects on wrong choices
